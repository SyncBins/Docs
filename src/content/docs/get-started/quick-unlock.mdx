The recovery phrase is 12 words for a reason — strong entropy, no shortcuts. But re-deriving the master key from it takes ~700ms (Argon2id with 64MB memory). Fine when setting up a new device; tedious every single time the browser idle-locks.

The PIN is the shortcut.

## What it does

When you set a PIN, the device:

<Steps>
1. Wraps your already-unlocked master key with a fresh Argon2id pass keyed on the PIN.
2. Stores the wrapped blob in IndexedDB (or its equivalent on the platform).
3. On next unlock, prompts for the PIN instead of the 12 words.
</Steps>

Re-deriving from a 6-digit PIN is ~80ms vs ~700ms from the phrase. Difference between "unlock is instant" and "unlock is noticeable."

<Aside type="note" title="The phrase still works">
The PIN doesn't replace your recovery phrase — it sits alongside it. You can always fall back to "Unlock with recovery phrase" on the lock screen, and you must do so on every new device.
</Aside>

## Setting one up

Settings → Encryption → **Set quick-unlock PIN**. Pick 6 or more digits. We enforce:

- Minimum 6 digits, no maximum
- No straight runs (`123456`, `654321`)
- No more than 3 repeating digits in a row (`111456` is OK; `111156` is not)
- Not your last 5 PINs

After confirming, the lock screen will offer PIN entry by default. The phrase option is always one tap away under "Other unlock methods."

## What it isn't

<Aside type="caution" title="A PIN is convenience, not a second factor">
The PIN doesn't add cryptographic strength. An attacker with a copy of your device's IndexedDB blob can brute-force a 6-digit PIN trivially. The PIN protects against **shoulder-surfing** and **casual access**, not against a determined attacker with disk access.

If your threat model includes someone getting your device while it's idle-locked, set a strong device-level passcode (iOS/Android lockscreen, FileVault, BitLocker) — that's where the real protection lives.
</Aside>

## Failure modes

- **Wrong PIN, 5 times in a row:** lock the device out for 30 seconds (doubles each attempt). Falls back to phrase after 10 attempts.
- **Forgotten PIN:** unlock with the recovery phrase, then set a new PIN. The old wrapped blob is deleted; no recovery flow needed.
- **PIN exposed:** Settings → Encryption → Reset PIN. Old wrapped blob is purged, recovery phrase required for one unlock to re-establish.

## Per-device, not per-account

PINs are local to the device. Setting one on your laptop doesn't set one on your phone. This is intentional — losing your phone shouldn't compromise your laptop's quick unlock.

If you want the same PIN on every device, set it on every device. We don't sync PINs.
