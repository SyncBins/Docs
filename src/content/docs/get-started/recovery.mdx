Five real-world things that go wrong. Find your scenario, follow the steps. If your situation doesn't match any of these, jump to [I'm in trouble](#im-in-trouble-and-none-of-the-above-applies) at the bottom.

## 1. "I lost my phone"

You still have at least one other paired device (laptop, tablet, browser tab). Everything is fine. You just need to revoke the lost device.

<Steps>
1. On a device you still have, go to **Settings → Devices**.
2. Find the missing device in the list. Click **Revoke**.
3. Confirm. The revoked device's session token is invalidated immediately and its wrapped-key bundle is purged on the next item you create.
4. If you got a replacement phone, pair it fresh — see [Pairing more devices](/get-started/pairing/).
</Steps>

<Aside type="note">
Revocation prevents *future* sync, not past. Items the lost device already downloaded are still on it. If the phone is wiped (Find My iPhone / Find My Device), the local data goes too.
</Aside>

## 2. "I cleared my browser / reinstalled the app"

This is treated the same as a brand-new device. You'll need to re-pair.

<Steps>
1. On a device you still have signed in, go to **Settings → Devices → Pair new device**.
2. Get the 6-word code or QR.
3. On the cleared browser, hit "Pair this device" and enter the code.
4. Your bins re-sync. Existing items download as the wrapped-key bundle updates.
</Steps>

If the cleared browser was your **only paired device**, this becomes scenario 4 — see below.

## 3. "I forgot my PIN"

The PIN is just a convenience wrapper. You can always fall back to the recovery phrase.

<Steps>
1. On the lock screen, tap **Other unlock methods → Use recovery phrase**.
2. Type the 12 words.
3. Once unlocked, go to **Settings → Encryption → Reset PIN** and set a new one.
</Steps>

If you can unlock with the PIN but want to change it (e.g. someone saw you type it), same path — Settings → Encryption → Reset PIN.

## 4. "All my devices are gone"

You wiped/lost every paired device. As long as you still have the 12-word recovery phrase, you can bring everything back on a new device.

<Steps>
1. Open syncbins.com on the new device (or your self-hosted URL).
2. Click **I have a recovery phrase**.
3. Enter the 12 words. The master key is re-derived locally; the device pairs itself into the account.
4. All your bins and items sync down. This can take a few minutes for a large account.
</Steps>

<Aside type="caution" title="Without the phrase, this scenario is fatal">
The server never sees your recovery phrase. We literally cannot recover it for you. If you lose every paired device *and* the phrase, your bins are gone. This is the price of end-to-end encryption.

Treat the phrase like the seed phrase for a hardware wallet. Paper, fireproof safe, password manager — pick at least two.
</Aside>

## 5. "I'm starting fresh on a new server"

You're migrating from syncbins.com to a self-hosted box (or between two self-hosted boxes). Items don't auto-migrate — you'll export and re-import.

<Steps>
1. On a device paired to the **old** server, go to **Settings → Export**.
2. Choose "Encrypted archive" (recommended) or "Plain JSON" (only if you're moving to non-SyncBins).
3. Download the file. The encrypted archive is unlockable only with your existing recovery phrase.
4. On the **new** server, run the bootstrap flow ([Self-Hosting → Quick start](/self-hosting/quickstart/#bootstrap-your-first-device)).
5. Settings → Import → upload the archive. Provide your old recovery phrase if encrypted.
6. New device IDs, new server, same content. Revoke or shut down the old server when you're sure.
</Steps>

## I'm in trouble and none of the above applies

Drop a line to **info@syncbins.com** with as much context as you can: when it started, what device, what changed recently, what error you see (if any). Don't include your recovery phrase or PIN — ever — even with us.

For self-hosted: also grab `docker compose logs syncbins --tail=200` and `curl https://your-box/api/health`. Half the issues are obvious from those two.
