## What SyncBins actually is

Think of it as **Telegram Saved Messages with proper bins**. A single-user place to drop passwords, links, screenshots, code snippets, voice memos, files, contacts — anything you want to hand from one of your devices to another.

It's not a team tool. It's not for sharing with other people. The only audience for your bins is *future-you* on your other device.

<Aside type="note" title="The mental model">
**Bins** are buckets you put things into. **Items** are the things. **Devices** are your phone, laptop, browser tab — anything you've paired. Items sync to every device.
</Aside>

## A word about your recovery phrase

During first-time setup we'll show you a **12-word recovery phrase**. It is the only way to unlock your bins on a new device. The server never sees it. We can't reset it. If you lose it *and* every paired device, your bins are **gone forever**.

<Aside type="caution" title="Take this seriously">
Write the phrase on paper. Take a photo of it and store it in your password manager. Both, ideally. Treat it the way you'd treat the seed phrase for a hardware wallet.
</Aside>
